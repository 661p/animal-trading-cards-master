Dingo

The dingo has rotating wrists, giving their paws a hand-like grasp. Dingoes can even climb trees!

  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus dingo
  • Average Length: 53 inches (133 cm)
  • Average Lifespan: 5-7 years in wild
  • Habitat: Central and northern Australia

Dingo colors range from tan, brown, black and white. They have longer legs, a lean frame, and are swift hunters. For smaller prey they hunt alone, teaming in packs for hunting larger prey. They communicate by growling, howling, and sometimes barking.
